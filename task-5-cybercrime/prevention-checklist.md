**Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students (India)**

1. **Use strong, unique passwords**
   Create different passwords for each account (college portal, email, social media). Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**
   Turn on 2FA for important accounts like Gmail, banking apps, and social media to add an extra layer of security.

3. **Beware of phishing messages**
   Do not click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown emails, WhatsApp messages, or SMS claiming prizes, internships, or urgent actions.

4. **Download apps only from official sources**
   Install apps only from Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown websites.

5. **Keep your devices updated**
   Regularly update your phone and laptop to fix security vulnerabilities and protect against malware.

6. **Use secure Wi-Fi connections**
   Avoid logging into sensitive accounts (banking, college portals) on public Wi-Fi. Use mobile data or a VPN if necessary.

7. **UPI Safety: Never share PIN or OTP**
   No bank or app will ever ask for your UPI PIN or OTP. Sharing it can lead to instant money loss.

8. **UPI Safety: Check payment requests carefully**
   Always verify before approving a “Collect Request” on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Receiving money does NOT require entering a PIN.

9. **Log out from shared devices**
   Always log out of your accounts when using college computers or cyber cafés.

10. **Limit personal information online**
    Avoid oversharing details like phone number, address, or college ID on social media.

---

**If you become a victim of cybercrime:**

* Report immediately on the **National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in**
* Call the **Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930**

Quick action can help prevent financial loss and catch the attacker.
