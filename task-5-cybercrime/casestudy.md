Ransomware Attack (Case Study)

A ransomware attack is a type of cyberattack in which malicious software encrypts a victim’s files or locks their system, demanding payment (ransom) to restore access. It is one of the most damaging forms of cybercrime today, affecting individuals, businesses, and even governments.

The attack typically happens in several steps. First, the attacker gains entry through phishing emails, malicious links, software vulnerabilities, or infected downloads. Once inside, the ransomware installs itself silently on the system. Next, it begins encrypting important files or entire databases, making them inaccessible to the user. After encryption, a ransom note appears on the screen, demanding payment—usually in cryptocurrency—to provide a decryption key. In some advanced attacks, attackers also steal data and threaten to leak it if the ransom is not paid.

Ransomware targets a wide range of victims. Common targets include large organizations, hospitals, banks, educational institutions, and small businesses, as they rely heavily on data and are more likely to pay quickly. Even individual users can be targeted through unsafe browsing or downloading practices.

The consequences of a ransomware attack can be severe. Victims may lose access to critical data, face financial losses due to ransom payments, and experience operational disruptions. In organizations, it can damage reputation and customer trust. In critical sectors like healthcare, such attacks can even risk human lives. Overall, ransomware poses a serious threat to digital security and requires strong preventive measures.
